About Qassim

Al-Qassim, renowned as the land of plenty and poetry, stands as a testament to agricultural excellence and cultural richness.

Its fertile lands yield a diverse array of crops, with dates reigning supreme among its offerings. This agricultural abundance has established Al-Qassim as a bustling center of trade and commerce.

Each year, the region celebrates its agricultural heritage with the vibrant Dates Festival in Buraidah, a spectacle that showcases the region's bounty and cultural vibrancy.

Beyond its agricultural prosperity, Al-Qassim boasts a tapestry of cultural heritage and natural wonders. From ancient archaeological sites to picturesque landscapes, the region beckons adventurers and history enthusiasts alike.

Revered in poetry and legend, landmarks such as "The Eyes of the Air" and "Jabal Ramah" weave tales of antiquity and intrigue, adding depth to Al-Qassim's allure.

A journey through Al-Qassim is a journey through time and tradition, offering a blend of culinary delights, historical treasures, and breathtaking scenery that captivates the senses and nourishes the soul.
